A brief description of Scrum

Scrum is a lightweight framework designed to help small, close-knit teams of people develop complex products.
The brainchild of a handful of software engineers working together in the late 20th Century, scrum has gained the
most traction in the technology sector, but it is not inherently technical and you can easily adapt the tools and
practices described in this book to other industries. You can use scrum to build a better mousetrap, for example, or
to run the marketing division of a puppy chow company. You can even use it to collaborate on writing a book we did.
A scrum team typically consists of around seven people who work together in short, sustainable bursts of activity cal-
led sprints, with plenty of time for review and reflection built in. One of the mantras of scrum is inspect and adapt,
and scrum teams are characterized by an intense focus on continuous improvementâ€”of their process, but also of the
product. This tiny book is a just-the-facts-maam introduction to the various moving parts of scrum: the various roles,
artifacts and events that occupy the sprint cycle.
